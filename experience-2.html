The three main ethnic groups cuisines

Malaysia has often been described as heaven for food lovers both local and abroad. The cuisine reflects the country’s multi-ethnic population where the signature dishes of various ethnic groups have soared to even greater heights thanks to the multiple ethnic influences that are uniquely Malaysian. The three main ethnic groups—Malay, Chinese and Indian—provide most Malaysians and tourists with their fill but for food connoisseurs, they can also choose from a number of different styles as well such as Western, Nyonya, Portuguese, Thai and more. Several international fast food chains can be found operating here as well, a perfect complement to the local food bazaars and road side stalls.

1) Malay

Malay food is often times described as spicy and flavourful as it utilizes a rich mixture of spices and herbs. Malay dishes can be distinguished into a few methods of cooking namely masak merah (tomato sauce), masak lemak (coconut milk), masak asam (sour-ish tamarind) and masak pedas (spicy). Some of the popular dishes that you wouldn’t want to miss include nasi lemak (coconut milk steamed rice), sambal belacan (shrimp paste with pounded chillies), beef rendang (dried beef curry), satay (barbecued skewered meat) and serunding (meat floss).

Limkokwing Creativity Library

The unique library occupying three floors presents the university’s achievements and its founder’s contributions over three decades. It traces local and international campaigns and projects undertaken by the university in fields such as peace promotion, international relations, humanitarian cause and creative communications.

Through pictorial panels, the library provides an insight into the power of creativity and the outcome of innovation. Visitors embark upon a thought-provoking journey to refreshing horizons covering nation-building, socio-economic development, innovative problem-solving, corporate identity, publishing, education, brand promotion, tourism and lifestyle change.

The library was declared open by the Former Prime Minister, Dato’ Seri Abdullah Ahmad Badawi, in October 2004. The inauguration coincided with the official opening of the university and the Malaysia Design Innovation Centre.

One World Club

One World Club is a recreation club for students with a most unique setting based on the theme of recycled junk art. It is where creative minds from all corners of the world interact and learn from one another.

Junk can be recycled to make amazing art pieces. This is clearly demonstrated to a point of brilliance at One World Club. Designed to be Cyberjaya’s hottest recreational haunt, the club blends in with the rest of the Limkokwing campus in theme and tone. Dark colours dominate and exquisitely crafted sculptures that make up the main centerpiece stand in stark contrast. At the same time, they fuse splendidly with the background.

These display items are original works executed by Limkokwing students, who are equally adept at creating a distinctive style of furnishing and interior decoration.

The One World Club provides a unique ambiance for students to relax, network, play pool or foosball or use the internet. Students are allowed to bring in food and beverages that has been purchased from Wings Cafe.
